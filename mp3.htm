　EM Optimization Lab

Established in 2013, the EM Optimization Lab is a research group that specializes in chipless RFID, antennas, microwave and millimeter-wave circuits, and engineering data analysis/mining in the National Taipei University of Technology, Taipei, Taiwan. We carried out several academic or industrial projects, including inkjet-printing technology, the implementation of chipless RFID in real-world applications, RF energy harvesting, wearable and implantable antennas, reconfigurable microwave devices, multiobjective optimization methods, vehicular radar antennas at 80 GHz, and optically transparent antennas.

The EM Optimization Lab has received numerous academic honors. The principal investigator, Dr. Chen, was a recipient of the Outstanding Reviewers Award from the IEEE ANTENNAS AND WIRELESS PROPAGATION LETTERS for the period 2017–2022, the Outstanding Reviewers Award from the IEEE TRANSACTIONS ON ANTENNAS AND PROPAGATION in 2019–2021, the Future Tech Award from the Ministry of Science and Technology (MOST), Taiwan, in 2021, the Outstanding Research Award from NTUT in 2019, the Dr. Shechtman Young Researcher Award from NTUT in 2018, the Outstanding Research Award from the College of Electrical Engineering and Computer Science (EECS), NTUT, in 2018 and 2019, respectively, and the Remarkable Teaching Award from the College of EECS, NTUT, in 2020. Research assistants have also received several honors, including the College Student Research Creativity Award and College Student Research Project Funding from the MOST, and numerous prizes from the annual academic special project competition. We have exciting research opportunities for graduate and undergraduate students. Interested students should contact Prof. Chen to indicate interest.
